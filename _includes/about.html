About me

I am originally from Candeleda, a small village close to the mountains in the center of Spain. I moved to Madrid to start the Bachelor on Computer Science at Universidad Carlos III, where I also completed my Master in Artificial Intelligence.

In 2021 I obtained my PhD under the supervision of Professors Susana Fernandez and Daniel Borrajo with the Planning and Learning Group, also at Universidad Carlos III. The title of my thesis is "Goal Reasoning for Autonomous Agents using Automated Planning", where we study how planning can enhance the autonomy of agents.

During the PhD I was involved in several research projects, as well as in teaching tasks. I also visited other research institutions such as RMIT University in Melbourne (Australia), where I collaborated with Prof. Sebastian Sardina.

Currently, I am working at the J.P. Morgan AI Research Lab in London.
